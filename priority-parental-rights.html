4-Day School Week Needs, Oversight

Ward 9 families have a right to know what's happening in their children's schools — and a right to be heard.

Brandon's Position

The Full Plan

The best schools I've ever seen treat parents as partners, not obstacles. When families are informed, engaged, and welcomed into the conversation, students do better. It's that simple. I will work to make Ward 9 schools places where parents feel respected and included.

Parental involvement in education is one of the strongest predictors of student success. Yet too often, school systems make it difficult for families to get basic information — about what's being taught, how their child is performing, or how important decisions are being made.

Brandon believes parents have a fundamental right to know what is happening in their children's classrooms, including curriculum content, disciplinary incidents, and health-related matters. The board should be a vehicle for transparency, not a barrier to it.

Brandon also believes that meaningful parental involvement means more than showing up to an annual open house. It means creating real channels — in Ward 9 specifically — where parents can raise concerns, ask questions, and receive timely, honest responses from the people making decisions.

Specific Commitments

01

Curriculum transparency. Brandon will advocate for clear, accessible information about what is being taught in Ward 9 schools so families can stay engaged in their child's education.

02

Timely communication on disciplinary matters. Parents should be promptly informed when their child is involved in a disciplinary situation — not left in the dark.

03

Ward 9 parent advisory input. Brandon will work to create or strengthen formal structures that allow Ward 9 parents to provide input on school policy before decisions are made.

04

Open and accessible board participation. Brandon will make himself available to Ward 9 families — not just at official meetings — so parents always have a point of contact on the school board.
